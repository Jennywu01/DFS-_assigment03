Liu Xian (1915-1990) was a pioneer of China's emerging printmaking movement. In his creation, he always adhered to the teachings of Mr. Lu Xun, adhered to the path of realism, and paid attention to the working people and social real problems. In 1934, he went to Japan to study under the tutelage of Yunichi Hiratsuka, a famous Japanese printmaker, specializing in black and white woodcuts. His works are characterized by bold composition, rigorous structure, graceful lines, strong black and white contrast, delicate and full of vitality, forming a unique printmaking style.

Mr. Liu Xian, a famous printmaker , passed away in 1990. After the founding of New China, Liu Xian served as the director of the research department of the National Art Museum of China, and made important contributions to the museum's collection, research and foreign exchanges. This year coincides with the 50th anniversary of the establishment of the National Art Museum of China. The National Art Museum of China specially held an exhibition of Liu Xian's prints to express the respect and memory of the art museum's predecessors.
